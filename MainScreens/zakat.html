**ALL INFORMATION IS FROM NATIONAL ZAKAT FOUNDATION** What is Zakat? Zakat is the third pillar of Islam, an obligatory charity that every Muslim who meets the requirements is required to pay annually. Zakat is paid by those who are wealthy for the benefit of the poor and needy. With Zakat, you can provide food, shelter, education, healthcare, and other basic necessities to those in need. Zakat is a unique form of social welfare ensuring the most vulnerable members of the Muslim community are taken care of. The ultimate aim is to strengthen the faith and provide a safety net for a set category of Muslims. There are 2 types of Zakat: Zakat Al Maal and Zakat Al Fitr
Zakat Al Maal Zakat Al Maal commonly referred to as “Zakat on Wealth” is a required act of worship in Islam that entails giving away a portion of one's income to benefit those who are less fortunate. It is calculated as 2.5% of an individual's wealth.
Zakat Al Fitr Zakat-al-Fitr refers to the charity given at the end of Ramadan's last fast. It is compulsory for every Muslim regardless of age or gender to give Zakat-al Fitr before Eid Salah.
